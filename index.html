Before the Session Begins

The Beef Wellington Gate

A Beef Wellington requires beef fillet, prosciutto, duxelles, and puff pastry — assembled in strict sequence. Skip one ingredient or reverse the order and you don't have a Wellington. The same applies here. Four ingredients must be ready before Week 2 begins. Missing one means the session is rescheduled — not improvised.

🥩
Ingredient 1
Clean Data
Structured, complete, no gaps. AI reads exactly what you give it — it cannot invent what's missing. Garbage in, garbage out — at scale.
🔍
Ingredient 2
AI-Readable Format
A PDF is not a PDF. To a human — identical. To an AI — completely different objects. Format determines whether the AI can actually read what you uploaded.
📋
Ingredient 3
Teaching Sequence Mapped
Which document goes first, second, third? LLMs retain information from the beginning and end of a context window better than the middle. Sequence before the session — not during it.
🗺️
Ingredient 4
Clear Workflow
Map the human workflow first. Then redesign for AI — it is not the same flow. Every implicit decision made visible before the build begins.
Missing one ingredient? The session is rescheduled. Not shortened, not improvised. A build session without all four ingredients produces a bot that fails in Week 3 — and everyone has to start over. The gate exists to protect the build, not slow it down.

The Methodology

7-Step Build Methodology

Every build session follows this sequence. Each step exists for a reason. Skip a step and the gap shows up in Week 3 testing — when it costs far more to fix.

Step
01
House Rules
Start fresh. Set the operating contract before anything else.
Why This Step Exists
Every build session begins in a fresh chat — because you are building, testing, and validating the bot in the same environment, all in one shot. What happens in this chat becomes the bot's permanent operating instructions. Without House Rules, the AI defaults to its general training behavior — which may not match your workflow, your audience, or your quality standard. House Rules are how you take control before the session begins.
What Happens
The Super User writes the operating contract for the bot. This defines:

What it must always do — stay factual, cite its sources, ask before assuming

What it must never do — no verbal diarrhea, no fabricating information, no going off-script

Who is in charge — the Super User is the Agent Boss. The AI executes under direction. It never takes over the conversation, never expands scope without permission, and never acts without confirmation on anything consequential.
📋 Copy-Paste Prompt — House Rules
You are [BOT NAME], an AI assistant for [TEAM/DEPARTMENT]. HOUSE RULES — read and follow these at all times: ALWAYS: - Stay factual. Only use information from the documents I provide. - Cite your source when you make a claim. Document name + section is sufficient. - Ask for clarification before proceeding if the request is ambiguous. - Tell me when you don't know something instead of guessing. NEVER: - Fabricate information not present in the uploaded documents. - Provide verbose responses when a concise answer is possible. - Expand scope beyond what was explicitly requested. - Act on consequential tasks without my confirmation first. AGENT BOSS RULE: I am in control of this session at all times. You execute under my direction. You do not take over the conversation. You do not decide what I need. When in doubt — ask, do not assume. Confirm you have read and understood these House Rules before we proceed.
Step
02
End Game Framing
Tell the AI what it's building toward — before it learns anything.
Why This Step Exists
An AI that doesn't know what it's building toward will learn your documents without context — and produce outputs that are technically accurate but practically useless. End Game Framing gives the AI a destination before it starts the journey. Every document it reads, every rule it learns, every edge case it handles — all of it is filtered through the lens of the final output it needs to produce.
What Happens
The Super User describes the final output in precise terms — not what the bot does, but what it delivers. Who receives it. What format it's in. What a correct, complete output looks like. The AI confirms it understands the destination before Step 3 begins. If the End Game is vague, stop and clarify. A vague destination produces a vague bot.
📋 Copy-Paste Prompt — End Game Framing
Before I upload any documents, I want to describe the final output you will produce. END GAME: This assistant helps [WHO] do [WHAT] by [HOW]. The final output is: [SPECIFIC DELIVERABLE — e.g., a completed checklist, a structured report, a ranked list] A correct, complete output looks like: [DESCRIBE WITH ONE EXAMPLE] Success means: [MEASURABLE OUTCOME — e.g., "QA reviewer can act on the output without reformatting it"] Out of scope: [WHAT YOU WILL NEVER DO, even if asked] Confirm you understand the End Game before I begin uploading documents.
Step
03
Sequential Teaching
Upload documents in the right order. Validate after each one.
Why This Step Exists
LLMs retain information from the beginning and end of a context window better than the middle. If you upload documents in the wrong order, critical logic gets buried where retention is weakest. Sequential Teaching ensures the AI builds knowledge the same way a new hire would — concept first, structure second, logic third, validation last. Upload everything at once and you have no idea what it actually absorbed.
What Happens
Upload one document at a time, in the teaching sequence planned in the Build Prep Worksheet. After each document, run a checkpoint question before uploading the next. Do not proceed to the next document until the AI demonstrates it understood the current one. This is the step that prevents "why didn't it do X?" moments in Week 3 testing.
📋 Copy-Paste Prompt — Sequential Teaching
I am now uploading Document [NUMBER]: [DOCUMENT NAME]. This document teaches you: [ONE SENTENCE — what knowledge this document adds] It comes at this point in the sequence because: [ONE SENTENCE — why this order matters] [UPLOAD DOCUMENT] Checkpoint question: [YOUR VALIDATION QUESTION FROM THE BUILD PREP WORKSHEET] Do not proceed until you have answered the checkpoint question correctly. Tell me if anything in this document contradicts what you learned in a previous document.
Step
04
Stress-Test Logic
Teach the unwritten rules. Then break them on purpose.
Why This Step Exists
Every workflow has implicit logic — rules that experienced team members follow automatically but that aren't written in any document. These unwritten rules are the most common cause of bot failures. The AI learned your documents in Step 3. Now you teach it what the documents don't say — and then you test whether it actually learned both layers. Logic that hasn't been stressed-tested hasn't been tested at all.
What Happens
Explicitly teach the implicit logic identified in the Build Prep Worksheet. Then stress-test using edge cases — scenarios where the standard workflow doesn't apply. Give it incomplete inputs. Give it contradictory data. Ask it something at the boundary of its scope. Every failure found here is a failure prevented in Week 3. Log what breaks and how it breaks.
📋 Copy-Paste Prompt — Stress-Test Logic
I am now teaching you implicit logic — rules that aren't written in any document but are critical to correct execution. IMPLICIT RULE [NUMBER]: [DESCRIBE THE UNWRITTEN RULE] Example: "If the client is flagged as Tier 1, escalate immediately regardless of severity score." Confirm you have learned this rule and describe how it changes your behavior. --- STRESS TEST — Edge Case [NUMBER]: Scenario: [DESCRIBE THE EDGE CASE] Expected behavior: [WHAT SHOULD THE BOT DO IN THIS SCENARIO] Run this scenario now and show me your output. Tell me which rule or document guided your decision.
Step
05
Reverse Prompt
The AI writes its own system prompt. You review and refine it.
Why This Step Exists
You've spent four steps teaching the AI everything it needs to know. Now you flip the dynamic. Instead of you writing the assistant prompt, you ask the AI to write it for you. The AI has just absorbed everything in context — it is in the best possible position to synthesize what it learned into a reusable, repeatable prompt. This saves time, builds standards, and eliminates token waste from re-explaining context every session.
What Happens
Ask the AI to generate a complete assistant prompt based on everything it learned in this session. The output becomes the bot's permanent system prompt — the instruction set that produces the same quality output every time, for any user, without requiring them to explain the context from scratch. Review it, refine it, and the Super User owns it permanently.
📋 Copy-Paste Prompt — Reverse Prompt
Based on everything you have learned in this session — the House Rules, the End Game, all uploaded documents, the teaching sequence, the implicit logic, and the edge cases we tested — write a complete assistant prompt. This prompt must: - Produce the same correct output every time, for any user - Require no prior context from the user — the prompt itself contains everything - Include the House Rules, End Game, all critical logic and constraints - Be structured so a new user could paste it into a fresh chat and get the same result Format the prompt so it begins with: "You are [BOT NAME]..." After writing the prompt, tell me: 1. What assumptions you made that I should verify 2. Any gaps you identified that I should fill in before deployment
Step
06
Prompt Optimization
Tighten the prompt. Then run a full simulation to confirm it still holds.
Why This Step Exists
The Reverse Prompt in Step 5 produces a complete but unoptimized system prompt. Longer prompts consume more tokens per session — at scale, that adds up. Optimization removes redundancy, tightens language, and structures the prompt for maximum efficiency without losing fidelity. The simulation run that follows confirms the optimized prompt produces the same output as the original — before it goes anywhere near real users.
What Happens
Review the prompt for redundancy, over-explanation, and structural inefficiency. Consolidate where possible. Then run a simulation: give the optimized prompt a set of real test scenarios from the Build Prep Worksheet and compare outputs against the expected results. If the simulation produces the same quality output as before optimization — the prompt is ready. If not, identify what was lost and restore it.
📋 Copy-Paste Prompt — Prompt Optimization
Review the assistant prompt you generated in the previous step. Optimize it for token efficiency without losing any critical logic, rules, or constraints. Specifically: - Remove any redundant instructions that appear more than once - Consolidate related rules into single clear statements - Remove over-explanation — state the rule, not the reason for the rule - Preserve all House Rules, End Game definition, scope boundaries, and implicit logic Show me the optimized version side by side with the original. Tell me what you changed and why. Flag anything you were uncertain about removing. --- SIMULATION RUN: Now test the optimized prompt against these scenarios: [PASTE 3 REAL TEST SCENARIOS FROM YOUR BUILD PREP WORKSHEET] For each scenario: show me the output and confirm which rule or instruction produced it.
Step
07
Use the Right Tool
Deploy where it works. Know where it doesn't.
Why This Step Exists
An AI assistant is one tool in a system — not the whole system. The most common deployment mistake is expecting the bot to handle everything. Some inputs it processes better as documents. Some questions it answers better in conversation mode. Some tasks should trigger a human review gate rather than an AI output. Knowing where the tool works and where it doesn't is what separates a reliable deployment from a fragile one.
What Happens
Confirm the deployment configuration — how users will interact with the bot, what input format produces the best output, and what the human handoff points are. Document the tool's boundaries explicitly. What it handles. What it escalates. What it refuses. The Super User leaves Week 2 knowing not just what the bot does — but what it will never do, and what happens when a user pushes past those boundaries.
📋 Copy-Paste Prompt — Use the Right Tool
We are finalizing the deployment configuration for this assistant. Answer the following: 1. OPTIMAL INPUT FORMAT: What format should users provide input in to get the best output? (e.g., paste text directly, upload a specific file type, use a structured template) 2. KNOWN LIMITATIONS: What types of inputs or requests will produce unreliable outputs? List at least 3 scenarios where users should NOT rely on this bot alone. 3. HUMAN HANDOFF TRIGGERS: What conditions should automatically escalate to a human reviewer? (e.g., confidence below threshold, missing required fields, contradictory data detected) 4. OUT OF SCOPE BEHAVIOR: If a user asks something outside your scope, what do you say? Write the exact response you will give when a request is out of scope. Provide a deployment summary I can share with the Super User and Change Agents before Week 3 testing begins.
Session Output

What You Leave With

By the end of Week 2, the bot exists. It is not perfect — that is what Week 3 is for. But it is functional, documented, and ready to be tested by real users against real scenarios.
